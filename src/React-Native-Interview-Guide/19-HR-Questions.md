# HR Interview Questions for Senior React Native Developers

---

# 1. Tell Me About Yourself

## What HR Wants

* Professional Summary
* Experience
* Current Role
* Career Growth

---

## Sample Answer

"Hi, I am Rajeev Joshi, a Senior React Native Developer with 9 years of experience in mobile application development, including 5 years of experience in React Native.

I started my career as a Native Android Developer using Java, which gave me a strong foundation in mobile architecture and platform-specific development. Over the years, I have worked on fintech, travel, entertainment, and founder-tooling applications.

My expertise includes React Native, TypeScript, Redux Toolkit, React Navigation, performance optimization, mobile architecture, and React Native New Architecture. I have also been involved in code reviews, mentoring developers, and collaborating with product, design, and backend teams.

Currently, I am looking for opportunities where I can contribute at a larger scale, work on challenging products, and continue growing as a technical leader."

---

# 2. Why Are You Looking for a Change?

## Sample Answer

"I am grateful for the experience and opportunities I have received in my current organization. However, I am looking for a role that offers larger technical challenges, stronger ownership, architecture responsibilities, and opportunities to contribute to product decisions while continuing my professional growth."

❌ Salary only

❌ Negative comments about employer

# 8. Where Do You See Yourself in 5 Years?

## Sample Answer

"In the next five years, I see myself taking greater ownership of architecture and technical strategy, mentoring larger teams, and contributing to business decisions while continuing to stay hands-on with technology."

# 9. What Are Your Career Goals?

## Sample Answer

"My goal is to continue growing as a senior engineering professional, contribute to large-scale products, strengthen my architecture expertise, and eventually take on broader technical leadership responsibilities."

---

# 10. Why React Native?

## Sample Answer

"I enjoy React Native because it allows teams to build high-quality cross-platform applications efficiently while maintaining strong user experiences. Combined with native knowledge, it offers a powerful balance of productivity and performance."

---

# 11. What Is Your Greatest Achievement?

## Sample Answer

"One of my biggest achievements was successfully leading performance optimization efforts for a large React Native application, significantly improving startup time, reducing crashes, and enhancing user experience."

---

# 12. What Is Your Biggest Professional Challenge?

## Sample Answer

"A major challenge involved diagnosing intermittent production crashes affecting a subset of Android users. By analyzing logs, reproducing the issue, and collaborating across teams, we identified the root cause and successfully resolved the problem."

---

# 13. Tell Me About a Failure

## Sample Answer

"Early in my career, I underestimated the effort required for a feature implementation, resulting in delays. The experience taught me the importance of better estimation, risk assessment, and communication."

---

# 14. How Do You Handle Pressure?

## Sample Answer

"I focus on prioritization, communication, and breaking work into smaller tasks. Staying organized helps me remain productive and calm during high-pressure situations."

---

# 15. How Do You Handle Conflicts?

## Sample Answer

"When disagreements arise, I focus on understanding different perspectives, discussing trade-offs objectively, and making decisions based on technical and business requirements rather than personal preferences."

---

# 16. Have You Led Teams?

## Sample Answer

"While my primary role has been technical, I have regularly mentored junior developers, conducted code reviews, helped with architecture decisions, and coordinated with cross-functional teams to ensure successful project delivery."

---

# 17. How Do You Mentor Junior Developers?

## Sample Answer

"I mentor through code reviews, pair programming, architecture discussions, and knowledge-sharing sessions. My goal is to help developers understand both the solution and the reasoning behind it."

---

# 23. Expected Compensation

## Sample Answer

# 24. If We Offer Less Than Your Expectation?

## Sample Answer

"I would evaluate the overall opportunity, including role responsibilities, growth potential, learning opportunities, team culture, and long-term career prospects before making a decision."

---

# 25. Why Should We Not Hire You?

## Sample Answer

"If the role requires someone who prefers working independently without collaboration or architecture discussions, I may not be the best fit because I enjoy working closely with teams and contributing beyond feature implementation."
